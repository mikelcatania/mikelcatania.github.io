I'm an aerospace engineer based in Darmstadt, Germany. I've spent most of my career in spacecraft flight dynamics at ESA's European Space Operations Centre, and I hold an EASA PPL(A) pilot license.

## Professional Experience

### Aircraft Conceptual Design — Independent (Feb 2025 – Oct 2025)

I took a break from operations work to pursue something I'd been thinking about for years: the conceptual design of a four-seater flying wing for general aviation, targeting EASA CS-23 certification. The bulk of the aerodynamic work was done with OpenVSP and VSPAERO — including optimisation loops, stability derivative extraction, and higher-fidelity runs deployed on AWS via Docker. I built a Python toolchain around it covering engine modelling, operational performance, and VSPAERO integration, all properly tested. The optimisation workflow was structured using XDSM, and I used Aerosandbox, XFoil, and NeuralFoil for supporting analyses. The design series on this site documents that project.

### Flight Dynamics Engineer — CLC Space / EUMETSAT (Nov 2022 – Present)

I work in the Flight Dynamics LEO team supporting EPS, Sentinel-3, and Sentinel-6 operations — station-keeping manoeuvres, orbit trend analysis, reporting, and contingency response. A good part of my time goes into improving operational processes: automating routines, making tools more robust, and building mission analysis capabilities in Python, Matlab, and Orekit. I also handle space debris conjunction assessments using CONANA, including collision avoidance manoeuvre planning and coordination with EUSST and CARA.

### Flight Dynamics Engineer — LSE Space / ESA-ESOC (Sep 2019 – Nov 2022)

I moved to the Orbit and Manoeuvre team, working on orbit determination, propagation, and manoeuvre optimisation with NAPEOS and MANTRA. A major effort during this period was coordinating the adoption of the CCSDS Navigation Data Messages interface with the Space Debris Office — defining the interface control document and rolling it out across missions. I also provided operational support for Aeolus and Swarm orbital products and developed Python tools for mission analysis.

### Flight Dynamics Engineer — LSE Space / ESA-ESOC (Jul 2016 – Sep 2019)

In the Command and Attitude team, I coordinated the development of common libraries and infrastructure for the command generation and attitude monitoring subsystems, working mainly in Fortran 90 and Python. I supported two LEOPs — Sentinel-3B and Aeolus — handling telemetry monitoring, attitude control, star-tracker alignment analysis, and fuel budget tracking. Day-to-day, I provided operations support for Aeolus, Cluster II, CryoSat-2, and Swarm, and served as Flight Dynamics Database coordinator for several missions.

### Flight Dynamics Engineer — CGI / ESA-ESOC (Feb 2012 – Jun 2016)

This is where I started at ESOC. I developed in-house tools for real-time spacecraft telemetry processing and numerical analysis — mostly in Python and Fortran 90, with some Mathematica and Matlab. Over four years I supported the LEOPs of Sentinel-1A, MSG-4, Sentinel-3A, and Sentinel-1B, and continued with routine operations for the Sentinel fleet. I also contributed to the command generation subsystem in Fortran 90.

### Mechanical Engineer — Helmholtz-Institut Mainz / GSI (Mar 2011 – Jan 2012)

Before moving into space, I worked on the structural and thermal design of the electromagnetic calorimeter prototype for the PANDA experiment at GSI. The work involved CAD design in CATIA V5, FEM structural analysis, cooling system sizing, and analytical thermal modelling of the piping system.

## Education

### Master of Aerospace Engineering — Universidad Politécnica de Madrid (2003 – 2010)

Speciality in Aircraft Engineering. My thesis was the preliminary design of a super mid-size business jet. During the degree I held a scholarship in the Propulsion Department, developing a numerical simulator of turbo-diesel engines.
